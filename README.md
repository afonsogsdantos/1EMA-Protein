# 1EMA-Protein-Structural-Analysis

Assignment for Biostructural Modeling class, Faculty of Sciences of the University of Porto (FCUP) 

### Goal of the project

The aim of this project is to perform a comprehensive structural and functional analysis of the **1EMA** protein, a mutant variant of the Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP) naturally originating from the jellyfish *Aequorea victoria*.

To accomplish this, we utilized macromolecular visualization tools and databases such as VMD and PDBsum to explore its 3D architecture, which was originally determined via X-ray diffraction with a high resolution of 1.90 Å. The project is divided into the following main stages:

* 
**System Characterization:** Identifying the physical components of the protein, which consists of a single polypeptide chain (approximately 27.23 kDa), an internal chromophore formed by the spontaneous cyclization of Ser65, Tyr66, and Gly67, and 95 water molecules that aid in structural stabilization.


* 
**Secondary Structure Analysis:** Comparing the assignment of secondary structure elements between VMD and PDBsum algorithms (identifying 5 total helices and 11 β-sheets) and analyzing the characteristic β-barrel architecture that isolates and protects the chromophore.


* 
**Stereochemical Evaluation:** Assessing the geometric quality of the structural model using Ramachandran plots to map the dihedral torsion angles for all amino acids, as well as specific plots for α-helices, 3_10-helices, and β-sheets.


* 
**Structural Classification:** Categorizing the protein taxonomically as a simple, globular monomer belonging to the SCOP class α+β.



The project involves understanding the stereochemical quality of crystallographic models, the molecular mechanisms behind biological fluorescence, and the practical context that makes 1EMA an extensively used biological marker for real-time cellular visualization.
